Dorothy Must Die (Danielle Paige)

In the book Dorothy Must Die by Danielle Paige, the main characters include Amy Gumm, Nox, and Dorothy Gale. The summary of this book begins when Dorothy Gale first visits Oz. After returning a second time, Dorothy craves the magic and power of the kingdom, forcing every civilian to work under Dorothy's command. When Amy Gumm gets sent to Oz in a tornado, Amy becomes an object of interest to Dorothy, and Dorothy sends Amy to jail. Mombi, one of the members of the Order of the Wicked helps Amy escape, but only for a price. Amy is forced to become a member of the Order of the Wicked but learns magic when Amy first arrives at the headquarters. Amy becomes the chosen one to kill Dorothy to stop Dorothy's unrighteous reign of Oz. After all, Dorothy must die.

Rating- ☆ ☆ ☆ ☆ ☆

From the reasons to give this book a five out of five, one of the reasons includes creativity. The way the author took a normal fairy tale and twisted the fable into a wickedly adventurous novel displays the amount of thought and creativity put into creating the book.

A significant quote:

This book included great quotes, one stating "Down is up, up is down. Good is Wicked, Wicked is Good. The times are changing. This is what Oz has come to" (55). This quote, minor but significant, describes the whole book, for the quote talks about how Dorothy took over Oz and caused the Wicked to become good.

#MurderTrending (Gretchen McNeil)

In the book #MurderTrending by Gretchen McNeil, the main characters include Dee Guerrera, Griselda Sinclair, Nyles, Ethan, Mara, The Postman, and Kimmi. The story begins when Dee wakes up in Prince Slycer's maze after her trial. When Dee got accused of murdering her step-sister Monica, she got sent to Alcatraz 2.0, a place where convicted murderers die. The Postman created an app so people around the world could watch these "murderers" die at the Painiacs' hands. The Paniacs, known as a personal group of the Postman's executioners, made these convicted killers suffer to death. When Dee accidentally kills Prince Slycer (a Painiac) while wearing a Cinderella dress, she becomes known as #CinderellaSurvivor all over the globe. This enrages the Postman, making Dee watch everyone she loves come close to death. Can Dee make it out of Alcatraz 2.0 alive, or will she end up dead like all before her?

Rating- ☆ ☆ ☆ ☆ ☆

Of the many reasons this book deserves a five out of five, the main reason includes originality. The author took a well-known island (Alcatraz) and transformed the island into the modern Hunger Games. This displays originality, for the author took a normal island and put a spin on it.

A significant quote:

A quote that provided much clarity in this book stated "Life on Alcatraz 2.0 sounded like a nightmare, and with twenty million spikes and a Cinderella Survivor hashtag, she practically had a sign around her neck that said HEY! KILL ME NEXT!" (38). This quote gives the reader an insight on what Alcatraz 2.0 feels like. The author provided this quote to inform the reader of what a chilling island Alcatraz 2.0 is.

Making Bombs For Hitler (Marsha Forchuk Skrypuch)

In the novel Making Bombs For Hitler by Marsha Forchuk Skrypuch, the main characters include Lida, Larissa, and Luka. This book started in 1943 when Larissa got taken away from Lida. Lida got shoved onto a train with little room but many people, going to Germany. When Lida finally arrived at the work camp, Lida faked her age and received the laundry job. The woman in charge of the laundry department, Inge, got used to Lida and gave her new clothes and better food. When the Nazis found out about this, they removed Lida from the laundry department and moved her to the bomb-making department. In order to help her country, Lida sabotages the bombs in hope that the Allies win so she could find her sister. Only question remains: Will Lida get away with sabotaging Hitler's bombs?

Rating- ☆ ☆ ☆ ☆ ☆

This book takes the reader back in time to the 1940s, which gives a very different perspective to the book. This book deserves a five stars because this book gives a clear image of the story in our heads. The author makes the reader feel as if they're actually in the book.

A significant quote:

A significant quote in this book included "I would sit there and listen with tears running down my face and think of my mother's truth: that beauty could be found anywhere" (99). This quote proves important for this quote tells the reader how even in times of despair, people found happiness and beauty in the world.
