Mystery Genre

The mystery genre is a type of fiction that follows a crime from the moment it is committed to the moment it is solved. The genre is also known as detective or crime novels. Mystery novels are often called “whodunnits” because they turn the reader into a detective trying to figure out the who, what, when, and how of a particular crime. Mystery writers drop clues throughout the plot to invite readers to join in the investigation. The hero of a mystery novel is usually a cop, private eye, or amateur sleuth with a strong sense of justice who faces a clever villain. Mystery novels are filled with clues and red herrings that keep readers guessing until the very end. When readers finally put all the pieces together, they feel a sense of accomplishment.
